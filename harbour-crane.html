Project summary

This project details the comprehensive structural modeling and dynamic analysis of a harbour crane utilizing the Finite Element Method (FEM). A custom 2D model was developed from the ground up to assemble the global mass, proportional damping, and stiffness matrices.

These matrices were subsequently used to solve the eigenvalue problem, extracting the structure's natural frequencies and primary vibration modes. The analysis included a static structural evaluation utilizing rotation matrices to determine the maximum nodal deflection caused by the crane's own distributed weight.

Furthermore, an advanced dynamic analysis was conducted by calculating the Frequency Response Functions (FRFs) for various input-output configurations. This included a rigorous comparison between the exact full FEM solution and the decoupled modal superposition approach to validate the frequency range accuracy.

The project culminated in a complex time-domain simulation of the crane's dynamic response when subjected to a moving load. By defining a specific kinematic velocity profile—acceleration, constant velocity, and deceleration over a 20-second transit—the time-dependent forces were projected onto the structural modes to evaluate the transient vertical displacement and harmonic oscillations of the crane's extremity.

Key contributions

  • Built a custom 2D FEM model for a harbour crane with global mass, stiffness, and proportional damping matrices.
  • Solved the eigenvalue problem to obtain natural frequencies and dominant vibration modes.
  • Performed static structural analysis using rotation matrices to compute maximum nodal deflection under self-weight.
  • Computed FRFs for multiple input-output pairs and compared full FEM and modal superposition results.
  • Simulated time-domain crane dynamics under a moving load with an acceleration–velocity–deceleration profile.
