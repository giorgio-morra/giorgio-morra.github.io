Vibration Control with a Piezoelectric Bender and an Active Electric Impedance

Piezoelectric shunt damping
FRF estimation & modal extraction
Data acquisition & signal processing

Mechanical system: cantilever beam with piezoelectric patch, magnetic actuator and accelerometer.

Project summary

Experimental vibration control of a cantilever beam by shunting a piezoelectric bender with an active electric impedance. The shunt is a negative-capacitance circuit (op-amp based, type BR) whose optimal layout and component values were derived analytically and then built on a breadboard.

The full measurement chain — signal generator, DAC, amplifier, current sensor, magnetic actuator and accelerometer — was assembled in the lab to acquire the system response in open-circuit, short-circuit and shunted configurations. FRFs were estimated with H1/H2 estimators and coherence checks, modal parameters were extracted through nonlinear least-squares curve fitting, and the piezoelectric capacitances were identified from the measured electrical response to tune the circuit. The controlled system achieved a 13.92 dB reduction of the first resonance peak, closely matching the 15.02 dB numerical prediction.

Key contributions

  • Set up the complete measurement and acquisition chain and acquired the beam response in open-circuit, short-circuit and shunted configurations.
  • Estimated FRFs with H1/H2 estimators and coherence validation, and extracted modal parameters (natural frequency, damping ratio ξ = 0.88%) via nonlinear least-squares fitting in MATLAB.
  • Identified the piezoelectric capacitances (C₀, Cpi) from the measured electrical response to compute the optimal shunt component values.
  • Designed and built the negative-capacitance shunt circuit on a breadboard and validated it against the ideal analytical response.
  • Achieved a 13.92 dB experimental attenuation of the first mode versus a 15.02 dB numerical prediction.
