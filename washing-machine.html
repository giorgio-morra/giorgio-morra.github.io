Project summary

Complete mechanical design of a 6 kg front-loading washing machine, developed from market analysis to manufacturing drawings. Starting from the design specifications — 84×60×45 cm cabinet, 40 rpm wash cycle and 800 rpm spin cycle — the loads generated by the eccentric laundry mass were defined and propagated through the whole drivetrain.

The powertrain was sized end to end: required power and torque at the drum, selection of a 610 W electric motor, and catalogue sizing of the Optibelt belt drive. The drum shaft was then designed and verified against static loads, fatigue, deflections and both flexural and torsional critical speeds, followed by the selection and verification of SKF bearings, locking systems, interference fits and the keyed pulley joint. The full machine was modelled in CATIA V5, producing exploded views and manufacturing drawings with ISO tolerances and surface roughness specifications, and the analytical stress results were validated with FEM analyses.

Finally, the structural dynamics of the suspended drum group were studied: the spring–damper suspension system was sized to meet vibration targets during the spin cycle, and two tuned mass dampers (one per degree of freedom) were designed with optimal damping to flatten the resonance peaks of the frequency response.

Key features

  • End-to-end drivetrain sizing: load definition at the drum (6 kg load, eccentric mass at 800 rpm spin), power and torque requirements, 610 W motor selection and catalogue sizing of the belt transmission.
  • Shaft design and verification: preliminary sizing followed by static, fatigue, deflection (rotations and maximum displacement) and flexural/torsional critical speed checks.
  • Machine elements: SKF bearing selection and life verification, locking systems, interference fits for drum and pulley, and static verification of the keyed joint.
  • CATIA V5 modelling and drawings: full 3D assembly of the machine, exploded views with bill of materials, and manufacturing drawings with ISO tolerances, geometric controls and surface roughness.
  • FEM validation: meshing strategy, comparison of linear and parabolic elements, and verification of analytical stress concentration factors in bending and torsion.
  • Vibration control: suspension system sizing and design of two tuned mass dampers with optimal damping ratio to cut the resonance peaks crossed during spin-up.
