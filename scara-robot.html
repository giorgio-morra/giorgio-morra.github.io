Project summary

This project covers the complete development of a four-degree-of-freedom (4-DOF) SCARA robot for a pick-and-place task: mechanical design, kinematic analysis, trajectory planning, dynamic modeling and validation, and finally the deployment of the same task on a real industrial manipulator.

The robot combines a base rotation (α), a belt-driven vertical translation on the column (z), a second revolute joint on the first arm (β) and a controlled end-effector orientation (γ), with link lengths of 350 mm and 250 mm plus a 150 mm end effector and a 200 mm vertical stroke. The full pick-and-place cycle is completed in about 17 seconds, compared to the 48 seconds required by the equivalent task programmed on the Epson T3-B401S SCARA through the Epson Programming Service.

Key points

  • Mechanical design: full CAD assembly of the base, vertical column, arms and end effector, with belt-and-pulley transmissions bringing the motion from each motor to its joint.
  • Kinematic analysis: derivation of the workspace and mapping of the singular configurations, used to verify that all planned trajectories stay away from singularities and within the joint limits (−160° ≤ α ≤ 160°, −150° ≤ β ≤ 150°, 0° ≤ γ ≤ 360°).
  • Trajectory planning: motion through the intermediate points of the task designed both with line-and-parabola (trapezoidal velocity) profiles and with cubic splines, respecting the velocity and acceleration limits of each motor.
  • Dynamic model: each link approximated with an equivalent two-mass model to write the equations of motion in MATLAB; the resulting joint torques are validated against a Simscape Multibody simulation of the full robot.
  • Payload sensitivity: actuation verified for payloads from 0 to 1 kg, checking that the selected motors can execute the task across the whole payload range.
  • Real-robot deployment: the task was re-parameterized and programmed on an Epson T3-B401S SCARA via the Epson Programming Service, comparing position, velocity and acceleration profiles in workspace and joint space.
